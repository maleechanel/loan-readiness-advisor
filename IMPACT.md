# Impact Statement

## The Problem This Solves

Black and Latino small business owners are denied loans at roughly twice the rate
of white business owners — not always because their businesses are less viable,
but because they often lack access to the preparation, knowledge, and guidance
that helps an application succeed.

A first-generation immigrant entrepreneur doesn't know whether to apply to an SBA
7(a) loan or a CDFI microloan. A woman of color starting her second year in business
doesn't know that her credit score of 610 qualifies her for programs specifically
designed to serve her. A worker who turned their skill into a small business doesn't
know that mission-driven lenders like Pacific Community Ventures exist specifically
for people like them.

**The gap is not in the quality of these entrepreneurs. The gap is in information.**

This tool closes that gap.

---

## What This Tool Does

It gives any small business owner a clear, honest, personalized picture of:

- Where they stand today relative to common loan criteria
- Which specific programs they are most likely to qualify for right now
- What specific steps they can take in the next 90 days to strengthen their position
- That mission-driven lenders exist who will see their community impact as a strength,
  not a checkbox

This is the information that a well-connected entrepreneur gets for free from their
network. This tool gives it to everyone.

## Connection to Pacific Community Ventures

This project was built in direct alignment with the mission of Pacific Community
Ventures (PCV) and their Radiant Data Hub.

PCV's President and CEO Bulbul Gupta has written: *"The power of technology —
especially AI — can and must be redirected to serve communities, entrepreneurs,
and workers, not just concentrated in the hands of a few."*

This tool is a direct expression of that belief. It takes AI — a technology that
has historically been built for and by the privileged — and points it squarely at
the entrepreneurs that traditional finance has overlooked.

PCV's Radiant Data Hub uses ethical AI and bias controls to help CDFIs lend more
equitably. This tool is built on the same principle: that when AI is designed
with intention, it can be a tool for economic justice rather than a replication
of existing inequalities.

## The Racial Wealth Gap and Small Business Lending

Key facts that motivate this project:

- Black-owned businesses are denied loans at 2x the rate of white-owned businesses
  (Federal Reserve, 2021 Small Business Credit Survey)
- Latino entrepreneurs receive 50% less in startup capital than white entrepreneurs
  on average
- Women of color receive less than 1% of all venture capital funding
- CDFIs like PCV exist specifically to fill this gap — but millions of entrepreneurs
  don't know these lenders exist or how to approach them

By surfacing CDFI programs alongside SBA programs and explaining specifically why
a given business qualifies, this tool makes the mission-driven lending ecosystem
visible to the people it was designed to serve.

## AI Safety and Ethical Design

This project intentionally demonstrates responsible AI use:

**Transparency:** The report clearly states it is AI-generated and not an official
loan decision. It explicitly tells users to consult a human advisor before acting.

**Honesty over flattery:** Claude is prompted to be encouraging but honest. If a
business profile has weaknesses, the tool names them clearly — because sugarcoating
problems doesn't help entrepreneurs get funded.

**No personal data stored:** The tool collects information only to generate the
report within that session. Nothing is stored, logged, or transmitted.

**Human in the loop:** The report is a starting point for a human conversation
with a lender or advisor, not a substitute for one.

**Bias awareness:** The tool explicitly weights community impact and mission
alignment as positive factors — intentionally countering the bias in traditional
lending models that discount these qualities.

---

## Limitations

- **Not a loan decision:** This tool cannot approve or deny a loan application.
  Actual underwriting involves documentation, verification, and human judgment.
- **Program availability changes:** Loan programs open, close, and change criteria.
  Always verify current requirements directly with the lender.
- **Credit scores are estimated:** The tool uses a range (1-5 scale) rather than
  an exact score. A hard credit pull by the lender may reveal different information.
- **English only:** A future version should support Spanish, Cantonese, Vietnamese,
  and other languages commonly spoken by California's small business owners.
